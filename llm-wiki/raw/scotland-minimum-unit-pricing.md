# Scotland — Minimum Unit Pricing (MUP) for alcohol

> Raw source. Captured from https://www.gov.scot/policies/alcohol-and-drugs/minimum-unit-pricing/ on 2026-07-13.
> Automated capture; the live gov.scot page is canonical.

## The policy

- Scotland sets a legal floor price for alcohol: retailers cannot sell below the minimum unit price. It is not a tax and raises no government revenue — the margin stays with the retailer.
- **Current rate: 65p per unit** of alcohol. The original rate of 50p per unit applied from the policy's introduction in 2018. The Scottish Parliament voted in April 2024 to continue the policy (which had been subject to a sunset provision) and increase the rate; the 65p rate took effect in 2024.
- Legal basis: the Alcohol (Minimum Pricing) (Scotland) Act 2012 (the page references the policy operating "by law").

## How the floor price is calculated

Minimum price = rate (£0.65) × ABV × volume in litres × 100 (i.e. 65p per unit, where a unit is 10ml of pure alcohol). Examples from the page, consistent with the 65p rate:

- Whisky, 700ml at 40% ABV = 28 units → minimum £18.20 per bottle.
- Wine, 750ml at 13% ABV = 9.75 units → minimum £6.34 per bottle.

## Evaluation

The page states research has shown the policy "has helped reduce alcohol-related deaths and hospital admissions."

## Context notes (added at capture, flagged for verification)

- Wales operates its own separate minimum-pricing regime (50p per unit, in force since March 2020); England and Northern Ireland have no MUP.
