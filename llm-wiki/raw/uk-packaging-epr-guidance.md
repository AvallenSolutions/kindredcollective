## Who EPR applies to

Organisations must comply if ALL of the following apply:
- Annual turnover of £1 million or more (based on accounts up to 7 April)
- Supplied/imported over 25 tonnes of packaging to the UK market in the previous calendar year
- Carry out qualifying packaging activities
- Are individual businesses, subsidiaries, or groups

## Classification: small vs large organisations

**Small producer** (either applies):
- Turnover £1–£2 million with 25+ tonnes supplied
- Turnover £1 million+ with 25–50 tonnes supplied

**Large producer** (both apply):
- Turnover £2 million or more
- Supplied/imported 50+ tonnes of packaging

For 2025 data: use turnover up to 7 April 2025 and 2024 calendar-year packaging weight.

## Packaging activities that trigger obligations

Organisations may need to act if they:
- Supply packaged goods under their own brand to the UK market
- Place goods into packaging
- Import packaged products in packaging
- Own online marketplaces enabling non-UK sellers
- Hire or loan reusable packaging
- Supply empty packaging (except to large packers/fillers/brand owners)

## Obligated producer duties

**Data collection and reporting.** Producers must track packaging by:
- Activity type (how packaging was supplied)
- Classification (household vs non-household)
- Material and weight
- Packaging class (primary, secondary, shipment, tertiary)

Large organisations report data every 6 months.

**Financial obligations.** Large organisations from 2025 onward must:
- Pay waste disposal fees (calculated on household packaging reported)
- Pay scheme administrator costs
- Pay registration charges to the environmental regulator
- Obtain PRNs/PERNs (recycling notes) to meet obligations

**Nation data submission.** Required for organisations that supply filled/empty packaging directly to UK end users, supply empty packaging to non-obligated or small organisations, hire/loan reusable packaging, own online marketplaces for non-UK sellers, or import packaged goods for own use then discard. First nation data submission deadline: 1 April 2026 for 2025 calendar-year data.

## Key reporting deadlines

Reporting periods vary by producer classification and year. Small producers' 2024 data was due 1 April 2025. Large producers report every six months, with nation-specific deadlines (England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland differ). Missed deadlines trigger late fees.

## Fee modulation

Waste disposal fees are modulated by recyclability: easier-to-recycle packaging attracts lower fees, assessed via the Recyclability Assessment Methodology (RAM). Illustrative base fees were published separately; final figures remain pending.

## Exemptions and special cases

- Charities are exempt from producer obligations but must register if acting as reprocessors/exporters
- Parent groups can report collectively or subsidiaries independently

## Support

Contact: packaging@defra.gov.uk. Compliance schemes can assist with registration, data reporting, and obtaining recycling notes but cannot pay waste disposal or administration fees.
