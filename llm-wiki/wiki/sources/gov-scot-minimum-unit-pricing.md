# gov.scot — Minimum Unit Pricing

**What this is:** the Scottish Government's policy page on Minimum Unit Pricing, captured 2026-07-13 — the primary source behind [[minimum-unit-pricing]].

## Summary

- Legal **floor price of 65p per unit** of alcohol for retail sales in Scotland (up from the original 50p introduced in 2018; Parliament voted April 2024 to continue the policy and raise the rate). Not a tax — the margin stays with the retailer.
- Floor price = 65p × units, where units = ABV × litres × 100. Page examples: 700ml whisky at 40% = 28 units → **£18.20 minimum**; 750ml wine at 13% = 9.75 units → **£6.34 minimum**.
- Evaluation cited on the page: reduced alcohol-related deaths and hospital admissions.
