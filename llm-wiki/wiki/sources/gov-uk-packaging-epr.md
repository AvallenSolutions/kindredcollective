# gov.uk — Packaging EPR Guidance

**What this is:** [[defra]]'s guidance on preparing for [[extended-producer-responsibility]] for packaging, captured 2026-07-05.

## Summary

EPR obligates organisations that meet **all** of: £1m+ annual turnover, 25+ tonnes of packaging supplied/imported to the UK market in the previous calendar year, and a qualifying packaging activity. The first listed qualifying activity — *supplying packaged goods under your own brand to the UK market* — is precisely what every drinks brand does, which is why this regime lands directly on [[kindred-collective]]'s brand members and their packaging suppliers.

Key mechanics:

- **Small producer** (£1–2m turnover with 25t+, or £1m+ with 25–50t): data collection duties.
- **Large producer** (£2m+ AND 50t+): 6-monthly data reporting, waste disposal fees on household packaging, scheme administrator costs, registration charges, and PRNs/PERNs.
- **Fee modulation:** easier-to-recycle packaging pays lower fees, assessed via the Recyclability Assessment Methodology (RAM). Final fee figures were still pending at capture.
- **Deadlines:** first nation data submission was due 1 April 2026 for 2025 data; large producers report every six months with per-nation deadlines. Late = fees.

## Why it matters to Kindred members

- Drinks packaging is heavy (glass especially), so drinks brands hit the 25t/50t thresholds at lower revenue than most consumer categories — a growing indie brand can become obligated earlier than it expects.
- Modulated fees turn packaging choice into a tax decision, making recyclability a supplier-selection criterion in the [[supplier-marketplace]].
