# gov.uk — No/Lo Labelling Descriptors Consultation

**What this is:** the DHSC consultation (Sept–Nov 2023) on updating the UK's no/low-alcohol labelling descriptors, captured 2026-07-13. This closes the wiki's longest-standing flagged gap — the "alcohol-free" threshold question.

## The answer to the standing question

Current UK guidance (voluntary, 2018): **"alcohol free" only at ≤0.05% ABV**, "de-alcoholised" at ≤0.5% (removal process required), "low alcohol" at ≤1.2% with ABV shown. So a 0.5% product — which pays £0 [[alcohol-duty]] and sits outside the Licensing Act's definition of alcohol — still **cannot be labelled "alcohol-free"** in the UK today; it labels as "de-alcoholised" or "low alcohol".

## The stalled reform

The consultation proposed raising "alcohol free" to 0.5% (aligning with licensing law and 27 other countries; England's 0.05% is among the world's strictest), extending it beyond de-alcoholised products, adding CMO/pregnancy and age warnings, and possibly legislating the rules. It closed 23 November 2023, was **overtaken by the 2024 general election**, and a further consultation was promised for 2025 — no outcome published at capture. Consumer research cut the other way: 85% preferred "alcohol free" to mean 0%/0.05%; under 6% accepted 0.5%.

## Why it matters to Kindred members

- The 0.05% bar requires expensive de-alcoholisation kit — a structural disadvantage for small producers that the consultation itself acknowledges, and a compliance trap for [[no-lo-alcohol]] brands importing EU products labelled "alcohol-free" at 0.5%.
- Guidance is voluntary but enforceable sideways via the Food Safety Act's misleading-labelling prohibition — trading standards, not HMRC ([[hmrc-compliance]]).
- Watch for the promised re-consultation: if 0.5% lands, a swathe of member products can relabel overnight.
