# gov.uk — UK Alcohol Duty Rates

**What this is:** [[hmrc]]'s official duty rates guidance (rates current as of 1 February 2026), captured 2026-07-05.

## Summary

Since the August 2023 reform, all [[alcohol-duty]] is charged **per litre of pure alcohol**, banded by ABV, across every product type. Headline structure (standard rates, £/litre of pure alcohol):

| ABV band | Beer | Still cider | Wine/spirits/other |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–1.2% | £0.00 | £0.00 | £0.00 |
| 1.3–3.4% | £9.96 | £9.96 | £9.96 |
| 3.5–8.4% | £22.58 | £10.39 | £26.61 |
| 8.5–22% | £30.62 | £30.62 | £30.62 |
| >22% | £33.99 | £33.99 | £33.99 |

Two reliefs matter to independents: **Draught Relief** (reduced rates, £0–£19.45, for 20L+ dispense containers) and **Small Producer Relief** (tapered discount by production volume, products under 8.5% ABV).

## Why it matters to Kindred members

- The banding creates a structural incentive for lower-ABV products — the £0 band below 1.2% and the £9.96 band at 1.3–3.4% underpin the economics of the [[no-lo-alcohol]] category that the PRD treats as a first-class brand type.
- Small Producer Relief is squarely aimed at the independent producers who make up [[kindred-collective]]'s brand membership.
- Rates change at fiscal events; treat the numbers above as *as of Feb 2026* and re-verify after each Budget (lint check).
