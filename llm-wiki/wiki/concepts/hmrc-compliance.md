# HMRC Compliance

**What this is:** the community's operational knowledge of dealing with [[hmrc]] beyond headline duty rates ([[alcohol-duty]]) — the approvals, returns and enforcement moods that dominate members' compliance questions.

## AWRS (Alcohol Wholesaler Registration Scheme)

- Required for any B2B alcohol sales; held by the company, not a person. Interviews run ~3 hours and are manageable with a prepared due-diligence policy (members have drafted these with Claude). Approval officers vary wildly — one arrived freshly transferred from VAT and had to be taught how drinks wholesaling works (2024).
- **Due-diligence scope is broad**: HMRC spot-checks confirmed obligations apply to *any* commercial customer — one member was asked for due-diligence records on Tesco (2026). Standard member process: New Trader Form + Companies House + Creditsafe + HMRC's AWRS checker + premises-licence register.
- Non-alcoholic brands don't need AWRS; VAT registration isn't required below the £90k threshold.

## WOWGR → APPA (2025 transition)

- WOWGR was abolished in favour of **APPA (Alcoholic Products Producer Approval)**, live 1 March 2025. Rollout was chaotic: wrong/duplicate APPA IDs, delayed letters, and APPA numbers initially not working on EMCS. Hard-won advice: keep filing W1/W5 until fully in the APPA system, and **don't let HMRC close your old warehouse licence until APPA works on EMCS**.
- APPA simplified reporting: monthly litres-of-pure-alcohol and litres-sold suffice (duty return by the 15th, payment by the 25th); detailed wastage/yield reporting dropped. Drawback claims went paperless (offset on the return, Excise Notice 207 s3.4).
- Rectifying/compounding **duty-paid** spirits no longer needs any licence (2024–25 rule changes) — but distilling from ferment still does, and bonded (duty-suspended) approval remains the hard gate (~3 months).

## Duty stamps — gone

- The Duty Stamps Scheme closed **May 2025**. From 1 May 2025, no stamp obliteration or HMRC notification needed on exports. (Historical pain preserved for context: CD-ROM label integration, stamps-touching-label rejections, de-stamping at £1.30–£4/dozen.)

## Bonded mechanics that trip people up

- **W5/W1 via ATWD**: HMRC doesn't proactively explain the process to new bonded operators. Duty is due at release unless deferment is arranged; VAT (unlike duty) can be deferred into the quarterly return — little-known (2023).
- **EMCS**: frequently down "on critical days"; keep manual fallback processes (2024). Movement guarantees no longer required between two GB tax warehouses; still required for exports (2025).
- Duty is payable on samples removed from bond — no blanket exemption. Fill tolerances: ~0.3% process loss for spirits; trading standards (not HMRC) police weights & measures, and prescribed bottle sizes apply ([[glass-supply]]).

## The R&D tax credit crackdown (2023–2025)

The archive's angriest recurring tax thread: HMRC retrospectively investigated and clawed back previously-approved drinks-sector R&D claims (£17k repayment in one case; East London Liquor's HMRC-triggered administration in Dec 2023 sharpened fears). Claims now need approved agents and "proper science" — new bottle designs no longer qualify. Members' advice: use reputable specialists on success-fee terms (they carry clawback risk — Forrest Brown and Datafox praised; Datafox won a 2-year appeal), expect the sector to be targeted, and escalate to your MP (one member did, on the group's behalf).

## Enforcement mood

- "Fit and proper" compliance visits (phone and in-person) focus on duty-fraud prevention: treat them as a detailed production tour, volunteer every number (2024).
- HMRC conduct is erratic on niche cases (a 2-year negotiation over duty on alcohol-preserved fruit) — budget patience, and use the British Distillers Alliance for escalation routes; a UK Spirits APPG duty inquiry opened in 2025 ([[alcohol-duty]]).
- Premises/personal licensing for D2C remains council-dependent: dispatch from a licensed 3PL usually suffices, but Amazon enforces premises licences strictly and rejects some bonded-warehouse licences ([[route-to-market]]).
