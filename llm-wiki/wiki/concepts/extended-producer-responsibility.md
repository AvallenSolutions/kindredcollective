# Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)

**What this is:** the UK regime, owned by [[defra]], that shifts the cost of packaging waste onto the producers who place packaging on the market. Guidance is captured in [[gov-uk-packaging-epr]].

## Who gets caught

Obligated if **all** apply: £1m+ turnover, 25+ tonnes of packaging supplied/imported in the prior calendar year, and a qualifying activity. The first qualifying activity listed — *supplying packaged goods under your own brand* — describes every drinks brand, so for [[kindred-collective]] members the practical question is only the thresholds.

**Why drinks brands cross the thresholds early:** drinks packaging is heavy — glass bottles dominate the category. 25 tonnes of packaging is a far lower revenue point for a bottled-drinks brand than for most consumer businesses, so a growing independent can become an obligated producer well before it feels like a "large" company. (Analysis; worth validating against member experiences once WhatsApp exports are ingested.)

## What obligation costs

- **Small producers** (£1–2m / 25–50t): data collection and reporting duties.
- **Large producers** (£2m+ / 50t+): 6-monthly reporting, waste disposal fees on household packaging, scheme administrator costs, registration charges, PRNs/PERNs.
- **Modulated fees**: recyclable packaging pays less (per the Recyclability Assessment Methodology). Final fee figures were still pending at capture (2026-07-05).

## The marketplace connection

Fee modulation turns packaging choice into a recurring tax decision. That makes recyclability a first-order criterion when choosing packaging partners in the [[supplier-marketplace]] — an angle Kindred can use: surfacing suppliers' recyclability credentials answers a compliance question, not just a sustainability one. The PRD's certification tags (Plastic-Free etc.) already point this direction.
