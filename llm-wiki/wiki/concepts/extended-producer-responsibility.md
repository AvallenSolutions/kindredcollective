# Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)

**What this is:** the UK regime, owned by [[defra]], that shifts the cost of packaging waste onto the producers who place packaging on the market. Guidance is captured in [[gov-uk-packaging-epr]].

## Who gets caught

Obligated if **all** apply: £1m+ turnover, 25+ tonnes of packaging supplied/imported in the prior calendar year, and a qualifying activity. The first qualifying activity listed — *supplying packaged goods under your own brand* — describes every drinks brand, so for [[kindred-collective]] members the practical question is only the thresholds.

**Why drinks brands cross the thresholds early:** drinks packaging is heavy — glass bottles dominate the category. 25 tonnes of packaging is a far lower revenue point for a bottled-drinks brand than for most consumer businesses, so a growing independent can become an obligated producer well before it feels like a "large" company. **Validated by the community** ([[whatsapp-kindred-spirits]]): members worked out 25 tonnes ≈ ~41,000 × 600g glass bottles, and EPR became the single most recurring regulatory pain point of 2024–25.

## What obligation costs

- **Small producers** (£1–2m / 25–50t): data collection and reporting duties.
- **Large producers** (£2m+ / 50t+): 6-monthly reporting, waste disposal fees on household packaging, scheme administrator costs, registration charges, PRNs/PERNs.
- **Modulated fees**: recyclable packaging pays less (per the Recyclability Assessment Methodology). Final fee figures were still pending at capture (2026-07-05).

## What members learned the hard way (2024–2026)

- **Cost reality**: ~10–12p per bottle from 2025 (Ecosurety estimate / The Grocer); "six or seven figure" impacts for larger brands; Fever-Tree launched legal action over the regime (2026). Compliance schemes members use: Ecosurety ("been great") and Valpak.
- **Liability confusion resolved**: fees sit with the producer/brand-owner, not whoever sells it; only UK revenue counts toward the £1m threshold; you must register and report by 31 May even below the fee threshold; missing a year is a criminal offence.
- **Supplier passthrough fights**: glass supplier Vetroelite surcharged even EPR-exempt small customers (£240/tonne cited, 2025) — the group pushed back collectively; some retailers/wholesalers also pushed EPR costs onto suppliers unilaterally.
- **Grey-area advice circulating**: a consultancy suggested excluding on-trade sales via signed customer letters confirming bottles don't leave the premises — while a large soft-drinks brand was reportedly under Defra investigation for exactly that exclusion. Treat with caution.
- **Admin risk**: PACKUK erroneously took 3 of 4 quarterly direct debits in one day (2025) — watch the billing.
- Retailer-side pressure runs ahead of the law: Tesco's packaging "red list" bans PLA/viscose capsules as recycling contaminants — one brand switched to rPET for a listing ([[glass-supply]]).

## The marketplace connection

Fee modulation turns packaging choice into a recurring tax decision. That makes recyclability a first-order criterion when choosing packaging partners in the [[supplier-marketplace]] — an angle Kindred can use: surfacing suppliers' recyclability credentials answers a compliance question, not just a sustainability one. The PRD's certification tags (Plastic-Free etc.) already point this direction.
