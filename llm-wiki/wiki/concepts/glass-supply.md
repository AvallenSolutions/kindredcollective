# Glass & Bottle Supply

**What this is:** the community's accumulated experience of sourcing glass bottles — the single most painful supply-chain topic in the archive, dominated by the 2021–2023 crisis.

## The crisis timeline (as lived in the group)

- **2021**: post-Covid shortage across UK/EU; brands lost Christmas sales to bottle unavailability; several switched suppliers mid-year (Bruni → Allied). 2022 price rises announced: Saverglass +9.5% (best), Allied +17–20%, Encirc +35% (worst).
- **2022**: Ukraine war removed ~half of Bruni/Erben's supply (Lviv factory); members shorted without warning. Croxsons demanded a 15% post-payment surcharge on a 15k-bottle order. Lead times hit 22–23 weeks; hikes of 60–80% reported; Allied announced +26.9% for 2023; Saverglass told one brand no decoration until 2024.
- **2023**: costs still climbing (a 70cl bottle from ~£0.70 to £1.25 in a year — supplier described as acting "like a cartel"); UK stock bottles scarce at 21-day lead times.
- **2024–26**: stabilised but structurally pricier. Red Sea disruption tripled container costs briefly (early 2024), pushing some sourcing to Turkey and Eastern Europe.

## Supplier landscape (community sentiment, date-stamped)

- **Consistently well-regarded**: Stoelzle ("delivered on every promise"), Vetreria Etrusca ("can't speak highly enough"), Vetroelite (reliable, though see its EPR surcharge dispute on [[extended-producer-responsibility]]), Rawlings and G&C Packaging as fast UK importers/resellers, Pattesons for miniatures.
- **Mixed**: Saverglass (bespoke quality vs "eye watering" pricing and year-long waits), Allied/Verallia (big price rises but delivery confidence), Estal (accommodating but surprise surcharges), Berlin Packaging (repeated comms complaints).
- **Persistent complaints**: Bruni/Bruni Erben 2021–23 — see [[supplier-intelligence]].
- **China sourcing** (Xilong, Rockwood, Uniquepak): mould costs ~10× cheaper (~$650 sample/$1,500 bulk vs £15–30k in Europe), but QC inconsistency (dirty bottles, fill/neck variance), upfront payment and 2-month shipping; consensus is workable for first runs/minis, risky at scale. Several brands onshored to UK-only glass from 2023.

## Practical knowledge worth keeping

- European bespoke moulds run £15–30k with 25–40k unit MOQs; coloured glass MOQs ~40–50k.
- No UK glass manufacturer will formally guarantee bottle suitability for pasteurisation — brands run their own trials (2021).
- Glass carbon footprint ≈1.25g CO₂ per gram of glass; reuse beats recycling (2022). Heavy glass is also why drinks brands hit [[extended-producer-responsibility]] tonnage thresholds early, and recycled-content now feeds PRN pricing.
- A 750ml-format bottle may legally be filled/labelled at 700ml (prescribed sizes: 100/200/350/500/700/1000/1500/1750/2000ml) — a packaging-cost workaround where the UK is a minority market (2026). See [[hmrc-compliance]] for fill-tolerance rules.
- Avallen's paper bottle (Frugalpac): 81g vs ~1–1.3kg glass, MOQ 5k, ~£1 bottle + ~£1 fill; filling is the hard part — one brand "lost 6 months" before using a filling partner (2022).
