# Fulfilment, 3PL & Bonded Warehousing

**What this is:** the community's map of where stock lives and how it moves — bonded (duty-suspended) warehousing, D2C fulfilment, and couriers. The most benchmark-dense topic in the archive.

## Bonded warehousing (UK)

- **LCB (London City Bond)** — the default incumbent; recurring cycle of price rises and service dips (2021: 17–19 days to land stock; 2024: "case price which is crazy") followed by recoveries; "not the cheapest, but understand why" (2025). Introduced a 10% fuel surcharge in March 2026 to significant pushback.
- **[[law-distribution]]** — best-value consensus for years, then exited all brands under 500 pallets with 30 days' notice (June 2025) — the biggest logistics shock in the archive; the group ran a collective tender in response, and LCB offered free landing to displaced brands.
- **Repeatedly recommended alternatives**: Chichester Bond, BWA/Belvedere, EHD (~1p/bottle/week storage in 2023), MSX (Totton), Seabrooks, Birmingham Bond; Cardiff Distillery opened ~100 pallet spaces to members (June 2026).
- **EU**: Amsterdam Warehouse Company the standing recommendation ("far better than Top Logistics"); LIS Logistics also used, incl. as importer-of-record on labels; Anker Amsterdam "best for Europe" (2023).
- Bond-movement guarantee costs jumped ~£100→£250/pallet mid-2023; brokering via Aon (~£1,000/yr minimum) beat insurer security deposits. Since 2025, guarantees are not required between two GB tax warehouses ([[hmrc-compliance]]).

## D2C fulfilment (3PL)

- **Codestorm** — the price-led default for years despite chronic complaints (missed dispatches, depot moves without notice, two break-ins); 2024 benchmark: £2.40 pick, £4/pallet/week storage, £3.66 next-day. £400/month tech fee (2026).
- **PHL Group** — "BRILLIANT" (2024 benchmark: £0.82 order fee, £0.22/item pick, £3.10/pallet/week, RM24 £5.07); watch notice periods (6 months for some, negotiable to 3).
- **Well-regarded**: We Are Fulfilment (2022–23 favourite; later mixed), Diamond Logistics (pricier, accountable), Haul and Store, PackSmart (the post-Walkers refuge), Kammac, MoveFresh, Fulfilment Crowd, Courageous Spirits (under-bond D2C, Shopify-integrated, Glasgow).
- **Avoid per members**: Huboo ("becomes a full-time job managing the mistakes"), Walkers ("a disaster"), Foda ("lost 20 cases").
- Benchmark: ~£7.50/case all-in pick/pack/courier (2023); consensus to bake delivery into unit price and sell "free delivery".

## Couriers (breakage is the metric)

- Measured smash rates shared: DX ~1.3–14%, DPD ~27% (2022, varies wildly by depot/period), APC ~35% on one Northern route; Flexi-Hex sleeves and double-wall e-flute boxes cut breakage dramatically (0 in 10,000 bottles one report; WBC pulpsafe "almost indestructible"), though Flexi-Hex scratches hot-foiled labels (~£1/wrap).
- Current consensus (2026): DHL most reliable for international/alcohol (esp. via InXpress), APC good for cases, Royal Mail cheapest for singles (2L spirits cap/parcel), DPD account-dependent (some accounts refuse alcohol). Packfleet was loved, then acquired by DHL and declined (2025).
- International freight: [[albatrans]] the standing pick; SupplyChain21 for bonded UK moves; JS Wines & Spirits and KC Shipping for Mexico/value; EGL avoided after $375/day demurrage attempts (2024); Hillebrand polarising ("90% of importers use them" vs "dear as poison").
