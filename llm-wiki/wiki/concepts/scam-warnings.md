# Scam Warnings & Fraud Patterns

**What this is:** the fraud playbook run against independent drinks brands, assembled from years of member warnings. Everything here is community-reported; treat names as "verify before trading", not verdicts.

## The recurring patterns

1. **Fake overseas importer ("419" variant)**: importer accepts your terms suspiciously fast, submits a large PO, then requires payment (~£500) to a "local lawyer" for an import licence. Repeatedly seen from Indonesia (2024).
2. **Big-buyer domain spoofing**: emails impersonating real procurement staff at real companies via lookalike domains — Musgrave Group ("musgrave-group.com"), Harrods ("harrods-store.com"), Emirates Flight Catering ("ekfcprocurements.com" and variants), Hyatt France ("hyatt-france.fr"). The tell is always the domain.
3. **WhatsApp buyer impersonation**: fake messages posing as a known retail buyer to extract stock — a real Speciality Drinks buyer warned the group her identity was being faked (2024).
4. **Large-PO-on-credit fraud**: big first orders wanting credit terms with no proforma; going silent when a phone call is requested (Laval Distribution, France, 2024; a suspected carousel-fraud approach for 8 pallets/week to Germany, 2022).
5. **Ghost distributors**: convincing websites, no verifiable trade — LalaWine (Singapore/Vietnam; sponsored a golf event that doesn't exist), Trading Post Global, Miasia Group, Sodiluc Groupe (all 2024–25).
6. **Trade-show attendee lists**: unsolicited offers to sell Prowein/BCB/NRB visitor lists are always scams (2025).
7. **Retrospective licensing invoices**: CLA press-clipping licences and Media Licensing Co's charges for using press logos on your site are aggressive but legally enforceable — budget for them rather than ignore (2024–26).

## Verification habits that worked

- **Check domain age**: whois lookup — the real emirates.com is 10,000+ days old; the scam domain was 26 days old with a GoDaddy contact (2024).
- Cross-reference DBT/trade-advisor scam blacklists and forums (WineBerserkers confirmed LalaWine); check the "shop" on Street View.
- Creditsafe / Dun & Bradstreet checks before credit terms; require proforma on first orders.
- AWRS due diligence discipline doubles as fraud defence — see [[hmrc-compliance]].
- Distributor "evidence" of marketing spend: validate invoices with the issuing party ([[route-to-market]]).

## Non-scam but reported losses

- **Ace Packaging** — members reported paid orders undelivered for months and a £20k loss; pursued through small claims/mediation (2023–24).
- Unpaid-invoice recovery that worked: gov.uk Money Claim Online (~£35–80 to file, CCJ then warrant; "reliable every time"), winding-up petitions, and the free Small Business Commissioner. Proof of delivery is the make-or-break evidence.
- Warehouse/cargo theft is real: bonded break-ins (2021), a 16-pallet wine truck theft in Leeds, containers stolen in Mexico (2023) — credit and cargo insurance (~0.2% of revenue for credit cover, ~90% recovery on failed debtors) repeatedly paid for itself.
