# Deposit Return Scheme (DRS)

**What this is:** deposit schemes for drinks containers — previously a flagged gap on [[uk-drinks-legislation-and-tax]], now filled from seven years of community experience, including the definitive cautionary tale.

## The Scottish DRS collapse (2022–2023)

The archive documents the whole arc in real time:

- **2022**: producer anxiety over cross-border fraud (English-sold stock redeemed in Scotland), separate barcoding, and Circularity Scotland's £365 registration fee. A member poll on joining returned 0 yes / 4 no; retailers (Waitrose) pressed suppliers to declare intentions; dedicated DRS WhatsApp sub-groups formed.
- **2023**: many small producers "reluctantly" registered to avoid Scottish delisting; the group organised an open letter to the Scottish Parliament (remove glass, align with the UK-wide scheme); the minister responsible faced a no-confidence vote; the scheme was delayed to 2024 and **Circularity Scotland went into administration**, stranding fees and prompting a reported £200m+ claim against the Scottish Government.
- Lesson the group took: don't build compliance operations around a scheme before its legal framework and operator are stable.

## Where DRS stands now (as of mid-2026 archive)

- **England & Northern Ireland**: scheme excludes glass; DMO (Deposit Management Organisation) appointed from April 2025, but **DRS obligations don't bite until October 2027**, with volume-based producer/wholesaler fees.
- **Wales**: opted out of the coordinated scheme and intends to include glass — divergence means anyone selling into Wales faces extra compliance.
- **Low-volume exemption**: product lines under 5,000 units/year escape fees/deposits/labels, but registration and reporting are still mandatory. Open question (unresolved in the archive): whether the threshold applies per SKU or per range.
- **Export labelling headache**: there is no clean way to combine multiple national DRS logos on one label — producers over-sticker per market; Ireland's DRS logo on UK-sold stock raised (unresolved) compatibility questions (2024).

## Relationship to EPR

DRS and [[extended-producer-responsibility]] are separate regimes that stack: EPR charges producers for packaging waste by weight/recyclability now; DRS adds per-container deposits and fees from Oct 2027. Both were explained to the community in plain-English guides published by [[avallen-solutions]] (2025).
