# Alcohol Duty

**What this is:** the UK excise tax on alcoholic drinks, administered by [[hmrc]]. Since August 2023 it is charged **per litre of pure alcohol in the product**, banded by ABV — the same basis for beer, cider, wine, spirits and other fermented products. Rates as of February 2026 are in [[gov-uk-alcohol-duty-rates]].

## How the banding shapes product decisions

- **Below 1.2% ABV: £0 duty.** Combined with the £9.96 band at 1.3–3.4%, this is the fiscal engine behind the [[no-lo-alcohol]] category — a structural cost advantage over full-strength equivalents (wine/spirits at 3.5–8.4% pay £26.61, at 8.5–22% pay £30.62).
- **"Per litre of pure alcohol" means small ABV cuts save real money.** Reformulating a 4.5% beer to 3.4% doesn't just reduce the litres of alcohol taxed — it drops the product into a band less than half the rate (£9.96 vs £22.58).
- **Still cider is the outlier**: £10.39 at 3.5–8.4% versus beer's £22.58 — a band structure that materially favours cider makers in the mid-strength range.

## Reliefs for independents

- **Small Producer Relief** — tapered discount by annual production volume for products under 8.5% ABV. Aimed at exactly the independent producers who make up [[kindred-collective]]'s brand membership.
- **Draught Relief** — reduced rates (£0–£19.45) for containers of 20L+ designed for dispense; relevant to members selling into the on-trade.

## The reform as the community lived it (WhatsApp archive)

- **Oct 2021 — reform announced**: bands cut from 15 to 6, taxed by ABV proportion. The group's instant read (later proven right): full-strength spirits get nothing, while RTDs and low-ABV products benefit — and "small producer relief pointedly excludes full-strength spirits".
- **2022–23 — the uprating saga**: mini-budget freeze, reversal, threatened ~12.6–14.2% RPI rise, freeze extended Jan 2023.
- **1 Aug 2023 — reform lands**: standard spirits duty +~10% (£31.64/L above 22%). One wholesaler reportedly quadrupled pre-rise stockpiling to £40–45m in July alone — a cash-flow game only big brands can play. A group lobbying campaign (WSTA template MP letters) preceded the Nov 2023 freeze to Aug 2024.
- **Feb 2025 and Feb 2026 upratings** went ahead (RPI-linked ~3.6%): £32.79→£33.99 above 22% ABV, £29.54→£30.62 below — members' real-time tracking exactly matches the gov.uk capture in [[gov-uk-alcohol-duty-rates]]. Per-bottle impact: +£0.336 on a 700ml 40% gin.
- **Duty receipts fell** at their fastest rate in 20 years after the 2023 hike (2024) — the group's standing Laffer-curve argument in lobbying; a UK Spirits APPG inquiry into duty policy opened 2025, and the British Distillers Alliance petitions for small-producer relief (2026).

## Reliefs in practice (harder than the guidance suggests)

- **Small Producer Relief** requires a formal HMRC certificate, is self-calculated, and in practice only helps sub-8.5% ABV products — there is still **no relief for full-strength spirits**, the community's longest-running policy grievance. Contract-producing at a larger facility may disqualify you (2024).
- **Draught Relief** needs 20L+ containers under 8.5% ABV connected to pump/gas systems — bag-in-box excluded; members sticker kegs "for on-premise sale only" to manage HMRC risk (2024).
- Useful quirks: liqueurs under 22% ABV made from duty-paid NGS can reclaim the rate differential (~£3.14/L pure alcohol, 2024); a member-built free duty calculator lives at shelfdrop.com/duty-calculator.
- Operational duty mechanics (W5 returns, APPA, deferment, drawback) live on [[hmrc-compliance]].
