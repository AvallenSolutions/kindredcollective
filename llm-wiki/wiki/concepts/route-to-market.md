# Route to Market

**What this is:** how independent drinks brands actually get sold — the distributor/wholesaler landscape, what it costs, and the structural squeeze on independents documented across the archive.

## The landscape

Members' informal top UK spirits distributors (2024): Proof, Mangrove, Cask, Marussia, Speciality Brands, Ten Locks, Love Drinks, Paragon, Spirit Cartel, Nightcap; agency-portfolio players Amber, Matthew Clark, [[enotria]]. Channel-mix poll (2024, n=44): most brands run "mostly distributors, some direct"; most have 10+ wholesale partners.

## What listings cost (benchmarks)

- **Matthew Clark**: ~£500 per SKU per depot (~7–11 depots ≈ £5k/SKU), negotiable toward free with full-depot commitment or a big account pulling the listing; "hurdle rates" and SOR fees added 2023. Sales-out data moved from free to a tiered paid scheme.
- **Venus**: £500/SKU/depot; post-Booker acquisition mandated BrandBank at £159/SKU/year (2025).
- Retail margin norms: supermarkets 25–30%+ (Aldi/Lidl 10–12%), premium retail 35–50%, independents/on-trade 60–75% GP, duty-free 60–70%. Brands should target 50–60% GP direct, 40–50% via distributor ([[finance-and-ops-benchmarks]]).
- Distributor retainers: consensus is tie any margin concession to guaranteed A&P investment, never just concede margin (2024). Always request underlying invoices as evidence of listing-fee/support spend — one distributor reused the same invoice across multiple brands (2026).

## The consolidation squeeze (timeline)

- **Jan 2024 — Diageo cuts direct supply** to ~30 wholesalers (from ~70) and sets a £2m ex-VAT floor for direct cash-and-carry supply; seen as existential for smaller wholesalers.
- **2024–25 — churn**: Booker acquires Venus (Tesco's orbit); Majestic acquires [[enotria]] (Mar 2025); LWC/Venus/Amathus aggressively poach accounts and staff from Matthew Clark, Speciality and Hammonds; Speciality trials 3-month performance delistings.
- **Feb 2026 — gatekeeping documented**: members reported large wholesalers (Matthew Clark named) refusing to list independents to protect Diageo relationships — a 30-case/month order blocked, and a listing refused even with 1,000+ Stonegate venues won; calls in the group for competition-authority scrutiny of fit-out contributions and marketing deals that lock out small brands. Smaller regional wholesalers remain far more flexible.
- **Feb 2026 — the opening**: Diageo's H1 FY2026 (organic sales −2.8%, NA tequila −23%, dividend cut, strategy review) read by the group as shelf-space and distributor-attention opportunity for independents.

## Alternatives and workarounds

- **DrinksBox** (member-founded, launched May 2026): bond-to-bar marketplace — stock consigned at LCB, brands set pricing direct to on-trade, no listing fees or wholesale margin, paid in 7 days.
- **Consolidators/enablers**: Tortuga (storage+invoicing+credit control, no sales function — cash-flow trade-offs), EzTenda (on-trade tender platform, member-founded, moved to £75/month subscription), Eebria (dropship, ~12%).
- **DTC-export platforms**: Tipple and BeMakers both got mixed-to-negative reviews (channel conflict, setup issues, 2024). A member-run co-op web store idea was analysed and shelved — a prior attempt (Lassou) sold ~6 bottles ("CAC vs Amazon/TWE is brutal", 2024).
- **Amazon**: outsells TWE/Speciality ~4-5× for many brands (2021); FBA lifts sell-through ~40% but needs premises-licence workarounds ([[hmrc-compliance]]), buy-box discipline vs Master of Malt, and careful case barcoding. Grocery rate-of-sale benchmarks: RTDs ~4–5 cans/store/week average, 6+ is success (2024).
- **US market**: "50 markets, not one" — three-tier system, importer-of-record needed (Park Street/MHW/Vista Imports), distributors are order-takers not brand-builders; costs run 3–10× first estimates; focus 1–2 states.
