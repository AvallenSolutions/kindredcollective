# RTD Canning & Co-Packing

**What this is:** what the community learned about getting drinks into cans (and contract bottles) — capacity, quality failures and the chemistry behind them.

## The leaking-cans saga (2022–2025)

The archive's most persistent technical problem. Multiple brands using the same co-packers independently reported cans leaking months after production:

- **Root causes established by members**: low-pH liquid attacking unlined can *exteriors* (tops/ends aren't sprayed like sides), which cascades across a stacked pallet once one can weeps; seam/lid faults from can makers (one brand lost a 100k-can run to an Ardagh lid-seam fault after shipping to the US); saline recipes are especially corrosive to liners (2025 case at pH 3.6 — advice: BCT liner testing, consider epoxy wine liner, check sulphates). Ball vs Ardagh performance differed by brand.
- **Best practice adopted**: store sample cans upside-down for 10 days post-production before shipping — catches seam failures early.
- For citric-acid drinks needing pH lift: trisodium citrate buffer (2024). Colour loss in pasteurised berry RTDs: lower pasteurisation temperature, ~0.2g/L ascorbic acid, or natural colour (black carrot) (2024).

## Co-packer landscape (community sentiment)

- **Bottled & Canned (Devon)** — the most-used and most-divisive: "most pro we've worked with" and low pilot MOQs (~1–2kL), but repeated leaker/carbonation complaints (2023–24) pushed several brands to Jolly's (sorbate dosing, no pasteurisation).
- **Consistently praised**: Canify (Bermondsey — bailed out a Christmas deadline in days), Can It (Bolton, small MOQ), Drinks Chef (small runs), Point Cocktails (small-batch blending), RED Distillery (canning + cream liqueurs + fermentation, repeatedly the flexible fallback), Get The Drinks In / Foreva Farmers (BRCGS AA, renewable-energy canning, 3,000L/SKU MOQ), Storefast (12k cans/hour; packs for Diageo and M&S), Whitebox (white-label, stubby steel cans).
- **Contract bottling**: Mallows Bottling (grew from new 2021 facility to 5 lines/100M+ bottle capacity with Development Bank of Wales investment), Hensol Castle (300+ products for 80+ customers), Giraffe Distillers (down to 35cl runs), ICB/Fortitude (cans 125–440ml from ~2,000 trays), plus 5cl specialists (Sloemotion, Broxburn, Young Spirits).
- **Cautionary closures**: Brew+Bottle/Renegade (repeated delays, then liquidation 2022), Alcohol Solutions (contract-term changes, closed Dec 2022), WeCan (quality issues), Brittains Beverages (good service, then insolvency 2024 — members scrambled to recover stock).
- **Can supply**: 2021's shortage (250ml slim cans, "the Wild West") made low-MOQ suppliers precious: Oasthouse (useful but "ropey" on colour-matching), NoMOQ (Ardagh-linked, repeatedly suggested for small batches), Bevcraft.

## Format economics

- Sleeving costs ~10–12p/sleeve + 10p application plus a logistical round-trip — digital can printing usually beats it (2023).
- 9L-case conversion conventions: 12×250ml cans ≈ 1 bottle equivalent; Diageo divides total litres by 10 (2023).
- Germany requires canned RTDs at 330ml and 10%+ ABV; France's sugar/premix tax (~€1.61/can at 5% ABV) killed its RTD retail market — reformulating to malt base or zero sugar is the only workaround (2024–25). US state bottle-bill deposit markings required from May 2024; UK canners generally can't etch can tops.
