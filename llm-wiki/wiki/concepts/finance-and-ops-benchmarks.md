# Finance & Ops Benchmarks

**What this is:** the hard numbers members shared with each other — the benchmarks a founder can't find in any published report. All figures are community-reported and date-stamped; sanity-check before relying on them.

## Pricing & margins

- **Worked pricing waterfall** (2024): from £40 RRP → ÷1.2 VAT = £33.33 → −25% retail margin = £25 WSP → −£9 duty ≈ £16 net WSP → 65% GP target implies ~£5.60 COGS.
- Margin norms: brands target 50–60% GP direct (40–50% via distributor); ~60% GP ex-duty is "attractive"; 20% producer margin flagged as unsustainable. Report **NSV** (net of duty, VAT, promo) as the industry standard — "duty isn't yours to keep".
- Retail/wholesale margin expectations: see [[route-to-market]]. COGS consensus: bottle, liquid, label, closure and inner pack are COGS; outbound logistics and storage are (mostly) overheads — IAS 2 cited; whisky makers capitalise distilling salaries into stock.
- Duty rises: always pass through in full — buyers can't dispute a duty rise ([[alcohol-duty]]). Raise trade prices annually (3–12% range), 3 months' notice, say "price movement". **Never discuss increase magnitudes with competitors — cartel risk** (competition law warnings recur across the archive).

## Capital & fundraising

- **Starting capital**: 26/26 respondents said £50k+ for a single-SKU spirits brand; £250k "realistic for phase 1" via SEIS; custom glass moulds alone £20–30k (2024).
- SEIS mechanics: max £250k raise / 30% equity → implies ~£833k+ valuation; typical UK SEIS-era valuations £1–1.25m; AML checks required (2025–26). Shares must be non-preferential for EIS; self-file EIS certificates rather than pay ~£500 (multiple members did).
- Fundraising craft (2024): target HNWIs first, minimum ticket (£10k+) filters time-wasters, crowdfunding tops up a round that's ~80% pre-pledged; mailing-list value benchmark ≈ £5–7 raised per subscriber (two crowdfunds compared, 2026).
- Exit context: spirits M&A multiples ~5–10× trailing sales (member-compiled dataset of 140+ deals, 2022); ~50k 9L cases and ~60% margin is where acquirers get interested (2024).

## Banking, insurance & finance ops

- Banking consensus (2024–26): **Starling** most trusted challenger; **Tide** widely panned (froze an account mid-raise); **Revolut** multi-currency but not FSCS-protected; **Mettle** low-hassle (won't serve spirits brands per one 2024 report — verify).
- Insurance: NFU Mutual for distilleries ("will actually pay out"); credit insurance ~0.2% of revenue recovers ~90% on failed debtors — proven in the Waverley and Farmdrop collapses; watch panel insurers exiting distillery cover (2022).
- Invoice finance: Close Brothers "know the industry"; Aldermore criticised (fees, sneaked personal guarantee); Ferovinum for stock financing; HYDR ~5% same-day.
- Insolvency defence (post-Farmdrop, 2021): retention-of-title clauses, watch payment-pattern changes, contra marketing fees proactively. Founders: pay yourself via PAYE with a real employment contract — it unlocked statutory redundancy from the government scheme when one member's brand failed (2024).

## People & ops

- Junior sales reps take up to a year to cover their cost; founders outperform early hires (2024). London ops manager (1–2 yrs experience) ≈ £45k (2026). Sub-£30k hires "very difficult" even for juniors (2025).
- Bookkeeping: £170/month (VAT-only) to £400–1,200 full-stack (2021). ERP: Unleashed powerful but needs a consultant (Outserve repeatedly praised) and 6+ months; Breww loved by smaller producers; Workhorse the lighter alternative.
- AI adoption (2025–26): Claude favoured for on-brand copy and even a self-built CRM/customer-service stack (<£200/month replacing Gorgias); consensus "rubbish in = rubbish out".

## Trade show ROI (the perennial question)

- **Worth it (consensus)**: ProWein Düsseldorf ("where business gets done"), NRB Manchester, Athens Bar Show ("best in Europe"), BCB Berlin for international distributors, Product Guru "Huddle" format for buyer quality.
- **Mixed/poor**: BCB London (heat and layout complaints 2025; "still finding its footing" 2026 — vs London Wine Fair the same week, busier), Imbibe in decline pre-rebrand, IFE "white elephant", Foodies Festivals ([[supplier-intelligence]]), Pub in the Park (entertainment crowd, "shocking" bottle sales).
- Consumer-show maths: BBC Good Food ~£5k all-in, 820 bottles sold, ~£2k loss but ~30p/sample — a sampling budget, not a sales channel; sweet, giftable, sub-£30 products do best. Set objectives (samples/listings/£) before booking; sponsorships without existing distribution are "a £50k billboard".
