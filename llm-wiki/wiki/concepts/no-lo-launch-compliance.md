# No/Lo Launch — Tax & Packaging Compliance

**What this is:** a filed query answer (2026-07-05) for a recurring member scenario — launching a 0.5% ABV drink in glass bottles under your own brand, at ~£1.5m turnover. Kept because the synthesis crosses [[alcohol-duty]] and [[extended-producer-responsibility]] in a way neither page states alone.

## Duty: £0, and the reliefs don't apply to you

A 0.5% ABV product sits in the 0–1.2% band: **£0.00 duty** for every product type ([[gov-uk-alcohol-duty-rates]]). A consequence that surprises people: Small Producer Relief and Draught Relief are irrelevant at this strength — they reduce a duty bill you don't have. The saving is already structural; see [[no-lo-alcohol]] for the category economics.

## EPR: turnover and activity are already met — tonnage decides

Of [[extended-producer-responsibility]]'s three tests, this scenario passes two automatically: £1.5m clears the £1m turnover line, and own-brand packaged goods is the textbook qualifying activity. **The only live question is the 25-tonne packaging threshold** — and in glass, 25t arrives fast (roughly 60–70k bottled units per year at typical glass weights, so most funded launches cross it within a year or two of traction).

If obligated at £1.5m turnover you land in the **small producer** band: data collection and reporting duties, not fees. The heavy regime (6-monthly reports, disposal fees, PRNs) starts at £2m+ AND 50t+ — worth knowing where the cliff is as you grow. Glass choice also matters prospectively: fees are modulated by recyclability ([[gov-uk-packaging-epr]]).

## Labelling: you cannot call it "alcohol-free" (resolved)

Per [[gov-uk-no-lo-labelling]]: at 0.5% ABV the product must be described as **"de-alcoholised"** (if made by removing alcohol) or **"low alcohol"** — "alcohol-free" is reserved for ≤0.05% ABV under current UK guidance, despite the £0 duty. Front-label ABV display applies. Watch the promised re-consultation: a move to 0.5% would allow relabelling.

## Remaining unknowns

- **Deposit Return Scheme** — the Oct 2027 scheme excludes glass in England/NI but Wales intends to include it; a glass-bottled product sold UK-wide needs a Wales plan. Low-volume exemption (<5,000 units/year/line) still requires registration. See [[deposit-return-scheme]].
- **Scottish retail floor**: a 0.5% product's MUP floor is negligible, but any full-strength siblings in the range hit Scotland's 65p/unit floor — see [[minimum-unit-pricing]].
