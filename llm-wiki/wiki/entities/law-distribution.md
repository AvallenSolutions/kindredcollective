# LAW Distribution

**What this is:** a bonded warehouse/3PL in Haydock (St Helens) that was the community's best-value consensus pick for years — and then, in June 2025, the cause of the archive's biggest logistics shock.

## The endorsement years (2021–2024)

Repeatedly recommended from 2021: "very good operation", "very good and cost-effective", weekly storage around a third of LCB's rate, "by far the best value" (2024). Members maintained 5–6+ year relationships. Caveats along the way: duty deferment terms not always competitive (2021), one member's formal dispute over damaged/incorrect deliveries (2022), "very expensive" for some smaller use-cases (2024) — but sentiment stayed strongly positive overall.

## The June 2025 exit

LAW gave **every brand storing under 500 pallets 30 days' notice to leave** — effectively ejecting the independent sector from its warehouse at once. The community response became a case study in collective action ([[kindred-collective]]):

- A poll identified affected brands and a dedicated WhatsApp sub-group formed.
- The group ran a **joint tender for warehousing and transport** on behalf of displaced brands.
- LCB offered free stock landing to those transitioning; challenger bonds (RED and others) picked up business — "opportunity springs from adversity".

## Lesson recorded

Even a long-trusted 3PL can exit your segment with 30 days' notice. Diversify bonded storage where volume allows, know your notice terms, and keep the [[fulfilment-and-3pl]] alternatives list current.
