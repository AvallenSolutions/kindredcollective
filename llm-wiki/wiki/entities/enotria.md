# Enotria (Enotria & Coe)

**What this is:** a major UK wine and spirits wholesaler that became the archive's standing case study in **trade credit risk** — valuable distribution reach paired with years of member-reported payment problems.

## The payment saga (community-reported)

- **2023**: months of distress signals — unpaid suppliers, invoices 100+ days overdue and unanswered, a reported ~£7m aged-receivables ledger, comparisons to the Conviviality collapse, credit insurers difficult; gradual repayment through spring. Members floated an informal "Kindred blacklist" with Enotria as exhibit A.
- **2024**: "consistently late paying" with repeated "processed for payment" stalling; slow payment continued through the year ("very very very slowly"); by November, members traded rumours of a Tesco acquisition amid speculation delays reflected distress ahead of a sale.
- **2025**: still slow (a September invoice chased into January); **acquired by Majestic (announced March 2025)** — members hopeful this fixes payment speed. Post-acquisition, consolidation/redundancy consultations reported (2026). One well-regarded individual contact was named as the exception proving the rule.
- Also noted: annual tasting-event tables quoted at £3–4k, called "ludicrous" for single-SKU brands (2025).

## Lessons recorded

- Distribution reach and payment reliability are separate axes — check both before signing.
- The community's early-warning value is real: Enotria distress signals circulated here long before trade-press coverage. Same pattern flagged Farmdrop (2021), Waverley (2022), Matthew Clark's credit-facility pull (2024) and House of Malt/NISA issues (2024).
- Defences: credit insurance (~0.2% of revenue, ~90% recovery), retention-of-title clauses, and watching payment-pattern changes ([[finance-and-ops-benchmarks]]).
